I am a Robotic Researcher working at the intersection of machine learning and robotics to push the boundaries of what robots can learn and do. My journey in robot learning began with my PhD at Imperial College London's Robot Learning Lab under Dr. Edward Johns, where I explored how machine learning can enable more effective robotic manipulation and control. I continued at Imperial as a postdoctoral researcher with Professor Antoine Cully, contributing to the DARPA-funded TRUSTLINE project under the Learning Introspective Control (LINC) programâ€”developing technologies that help robots monitor and understand their own performance and reliability.

My research spans from fundamental questions in robot learning to practical systems that work in the real world. I'm particularly passionate about developing machine learning algorithms that enable robots to handle diverse manipulation tasks.
